Convection

Convection is one of the main methods of heat transfer in the troposphere. Warm moist air rises above cold dry air and the water vapour in the moist air condenses to form droplets. Lots of droplets make a cloud. As water vapour condenses, it releases energy, which warms the air around it.
In konrad, convection takes energy from the surface and warms the atmosphere. If we stop convection in konrad, we will find radiative equilibrium.
