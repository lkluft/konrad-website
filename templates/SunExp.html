The Sun

The Sun is the closest star to Earth, about 149.6 million km away. Stars are balls of plasma, which produce energy by fusion. The Sun is the most important source of energy for life on Earth. The amount of energy received in any place on Earth depends on the location, the season and the time of day, with the tropics receiving more sunlight on average than the polar regions. The light coming from the Sun contains mainly ultra-violet, visible and infra-red radiation.

In konrad, we normally don't model day and night, but instead we use a constant value for the strength of sunlight. The value used is a bit lower than the average of day and night in the tropics (see the model assumptions ).

In this experiment, we reduce solar irradiance by 5 percent. This would be equivalent to moving the Earth 3.9 million km further away from the Sun.
